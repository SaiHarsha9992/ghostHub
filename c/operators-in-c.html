1. Arithmetic Operators (Basic Math)

These operators perform fundamental mathematical calculations.

Operator Meaning Example Output
+ Addition 10 + 5 15
- Subtraction 10 - 5 5
* Multiplication 10 * 5 50
/ Division (integer result) 10 / 5 2
% Modulus (remainder) 10 % 3 1
++ Increment x++ (adds 1 to x) x becomes 11 if it was 10
-- Decrement x-- (subtracts 1 from x) x becomes 9 if it was 10

2. Assignment Operators

These are used to assign a value to a variable.

Operator Example Same As
= x = 5 x = 5
+= x += 3 x = x + 3
-= x -= 2 x = x - 2
*= x *= 2 x = x * 2
/= x /= 2 x = x / 2
%= x %= 2 x = x % 2

ðŸ‘‰ Real-life example: Just like updating your bank balance after a transaction, assignment operators let you efficiently modify a variable's value.

3. Comparison Operators

These operators compare two values and return True (represented as 1) or False (represented as 0).

Operator Meaning Example Output
== Equal to 10 == 10 1 (True)
!= Not equal to 10 != 5 1 (True)
> Greater than 10 > 5 1 (True)
< Less than 5 < 10 1 (True)
>= Greater or equal 10 >= 10 1 (True)
<= Less or equal 5 <= 10 1 (True)

4. Logical Operators

Used to combine or manipulate boolean expressions.

Operator Meaning Example Output
&& Logical AND (both conditions must be true) (5 > 2 && 10 > 5) 1 (True)
|| Logical OR (at least one condition must be true) (5 > 2 || 10 < 5) 1 (True)
! Logical NOT (reverses the result) !(5 > 2) 0 (False)

5. Bitwise Operators (working with bits)

These operators manipulate numbers at the bit level.

Operator Meaning Example (a=5, b=3) Result
& Bitwise AND a & b 1
| Bitwise OR a | b 7
^ Bitwise XOR a ^ b 6
~ Bitwise NOT (one's complement) ~a -6
<< Left Shift a << 1 10
>> Right Shift a >> 1 2

ðŸ‘‰ Analogy: Think of these as controlling individual light switches (bits) in a row. << shifts all lights to the left, and >> shifts them to the right.

6. Miscellaneous Operators

C has some unique operators not found in many other languages.

  • sizeof(): Returns the size of a variable or data type in bytes. 
    sizeof(int) might be 4 on a 32-bit system.
  • &: Address-of operator. Gives the memory address of a variable.
  • *: Pointer-to operator. Used to access the value at a memory address. This is essential for working with pointers.
