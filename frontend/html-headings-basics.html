HTML Headings ‚Äì Organizing Your Webpage Titles

1. What are Headings in HTML?

‚óè Headings are used to define titles and subtitles on a webpage.

‚óè They make text stand out and help in **SEO** (Search Engine Optimization).

‚óè HTML has 6 levels of headings: <h1> to <h6>.

üëâ Example (Full Code):

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
  <title>Heading Example</title>
</head>
<body>
  <h1>This is Heading 1</h1>
  <h2>This is Heading 2</h2>
  <h3>This is Heading 3</h3>
  <h4>This is Heading 4</h4>
  <h5>This is Heading 5</h5>
  <h6>This is Heading 6</h6>
</body>
</html>

2. Output in Browser üñºÔ∏è

When you run the above code, the browser will display:

  • Heading 1 ‚Üí **Largest**
  • Heading 6 ‚Üí **Smallest**

3. Why Use Different Headings?

  • <h1> ‚Üí **Main page title** (should appear only once per page).
  • <h2> ‚Üí **Section headings**.
  • <h3> ‚Üí **Sub-sections**.
  • <h4>, <h5>, <h6> ‚Üí **Smaller headings or notes**.

üìå Headings work like chapters and sub-chapters in a book. They give your webpage a clear structure.

4. Styling Headings with CSS (Optional)

You can make headings more attractive using inline CSS.

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
  <title>Styled Headings</title>
</head>
<body>
  <h1 style="color: blue; text-align: center;">Main Title</h1>
  <h2 style="color: green;">Section Title</h2>
  <h3 style="font-family: Arial;">Subsection</h3>
</body>
</html>

6. Conclusion

  • Headings make webpages organized and easy to read.
  • `<h1>` is the most important heading, `<h6>` is the least important.
  • Use headings in order, like a hierarchy, for better design and SEO.
